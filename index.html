Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks (Poverty vs Healthcare)

Using the U.S. Census Bureau and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System from 2014

States that have a lower percentage of population in poverty also have a lower percentage of population that lacks healthcare.

For example, Connecticut has less than 11% of its population in poverty and less than 10% of the population lacks healthcare.

At the other end of the chart, LA has a higher percentage of population in poverty (about 20%) and also has a higher percentage of population that lacks healthcare (about 19%).
